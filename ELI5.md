# Twinfolio — Explained Like You're 5

## The problem

Grown-ups have money. They put some in the bank, some they spend, some they try to save for later (like a new house, or school for their kids). But most people don't have a helper who understands *their* money — they just get the same generic advice as everyone else, or no advice at all.

## What we're building

Imagine you had a **magic twin** who lives inside your phone. This twin knows everything about your money — how much comes in, how much goes out, what you're saving for.

You can ask your twin questions like:

> "What if I save ₹100 every week instead of spending it on candy?"

And instead of just saying "that's good," your twin **shows you** — like a crystal ball — what your piggy bank would look like in 1 year, or 5 years, if you did that. You can see the future change right in front of you.

## Your twin is honest, not just nice

Some helpers just tell you what you want to hear. Your twin is different — it looks at what you've *actually done before* (not just what you say), and tells you the truth kindly. Like:

> "Last time you got scared and hid all your candy instead of saving it — that's okay, but here's what would've happened if you hadn't."

It's not scolding you. It's coaching you, like a good friend.

## Your twin notices things, so you don't have to ask

You don't always remember to check in with your twin. So instead, your twin **watches for the right moments** and taps you on the shoulder:

> "Hey! You just got your allowance and you didn't spend much this week — want me to save some of it for your bike fund?"

## How your twin explains itself

Your twin never just says "trust me." It always explains *why* it's saying something, using your own piggy bank history — so it's not a mystery, it's a friend showing its work.

## Why this matters

Right now, this kind of helpful, honest, always-there money-twin mostly only exists for rich people who can pay a real person to do this for them. We're building it so **anyone** with a bank app can have one — for free, in their pocket.

## In one sentence

**We're building a friendly, honest little twin that lives in your banking app, knows your money story, and helps you make good choices about it — before you even have to ask.**

---

## What we build first, second, third...

You can't build a twin all at once — you build it piece by piece, like building blocks. Here's the order, and why each one comes before the next.

**1. Build the twin's "house" first — the plumbing nobody sees** — 🚧 half-done
Before the twin can do anything, it needs a place to live: somewhere to remember things (its memory boxes), and a way for you to even open the app and say "hi, it's me." The memory boxes are built and tested for real (they genuinely remember things now) — but the "say hi, it's me" part (login) isn't connected yet, so the twin still can't tell who it's talking to.

**2. Give the twin its crystal ball** — ✅ done
The twin can now do the "what if" math for real — if you save this much, here's what happens later.

**3. Let you actually *see* the crystal ball** — ✅ done
There's a real screen now where you see your savings goal, and watch the number change when you try different "what ifs."

**4. Give the twin a voice** — ✅ done
You can now *talk* to the twin instead of just clicking buttons — it listens, understands, and really does use the crystal ball from step 2 to answer you (we checked: it's not just making up numbers that sound right).

**5. Teach the twin to know you, honestly** — ✅ done
The twin can now look at a made-up example of what someone's *actually done before* and gently coach them — real money history isn't connected yet, but the "look and coach honestly" part genuinely works.

**6. Teach the twin to notice things on its own** — ⬜ not started

**7. Give the grown-ups (the bank helpers) their own window** — ⬜ not started

**8. Polish, so it feels like magic and not homework** — ⬜ not started

### Why this order and not some other order?
Each step needs the one before it to already exist — you can't teach the twin to notice things (step 6) before it can talk (step 4), and it can't talk usefully before it has a crystal ball (step 2) to talk about. We always build the *thing itself* before the *shiny bits around it*.
